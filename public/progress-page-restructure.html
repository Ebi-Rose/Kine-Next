Progress — Restructure Options

Four mockups exploring how to reshape the Progress page. All options preserve existing data; the differences are in hierarchy, discoverability and voice.

Current problems

  • One long scroll — no visual hierarchy. Lift history balloons with every new exercise.
  • 4-stat grid and StrengthTrend compete for attention before anything actionable shows.
  • Tools grid (1RM / Calendar / Photos / Trends) breaks the read mid-page.
  • No body, photos, or measurements surface — despite women-focused positioning.
  • Nothing answers "is this good?" — the #1 unmet need for new women lifters.

What competitors do

  • Table stakes: workout history, streaks, per-lift PRs, volume over time, measurements, lifetime totals.
  • Differentiators: Fitbod's muscle heatmap · Caliber's benchmarked Strength Score · Apple's Trends arrows (vs. your own 365-day avg) · Hevy's social PR moments · Caliber's first-class progress photos · Future's coach check-in timeline.
  • Gap we can own: most apps pick one lane — strength or body comp or consistency. Unifying the three for women × strength is the opening.

Principles check

  • All streak / score / leaderboard language has been removed — per principles #2 Guide, Don't Gate, #3 Closed Loop, and #14 No Scores, No Streaks.
  • "Is this good?" is answered against the user's own baseline (Option D), never against other users. Observation, not verdict.
  • Session counts are shown as neutral facts ("3 sessions this week"), not streaks with break-state shame.

Recommendation

  • Ship first: Option A if Body/photos are on the near roadmap (the tab is a natural home), or B if Progress should become a scannable hub and we can build detail routes.
  • North star: Option D — the "vs. your own baseline" framing is fully principle-safe and answers the core unmet need without introducing scores or comparison. Can be layered on top of A/B later as the hero card.
  • Borrow from C regardless: every block should lead with a verdict about the data ("Stronger by 8%"), not a label. Cheapest win, biggest voice lift.

Open questions

  • Do we build the Body surface (photos + measurements) now, or later?
  • Is a benchmarked Strength Score realistic without population data yet — or do we fake-benchmark against the user's own baseline first?
  • Should the lift history live on the Progress page at all, or move to its own route?
  • Does the Tools grid (1RM / Calendar / Photos / Trends) survive, or get dissolved into cards/tabs?
