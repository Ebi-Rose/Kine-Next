Philosophy

Key Principles

The foundational and expanded principles that shape every screen, every word, and every decision in Kinē.

💡

Revelation, Not Correction

Kinē speaks like a knowledgeable friend sharing something nobody told you either. She hasn't made mistakes — she simply hasn't been told yet.

  • No "make sure you" or "you need to" — every cue is framed as sharing, not instructing
  • Applies everywhere: exercise descriptions, cycle banners, error states, AI guidance
The difference
"Make sure you keep your back straight during deadlifts."
"Your legs, hips, and back all work at once — which is why nothing else quite replaces it."
🎯

Education at Point of Relevance

Micro-learning surfaces in the moment it matters — not before, not after. Nothing is front-loaded. You learn by doing, not by reading.

  • Breathing cues appear when you're about to lift — not in a separate tutorial
  • First-time education fires once per concept, then permanently retires
The difference
A 10-minute tutorial before your first workout explaining every concept.
Set notation "3×8" is explained the first time you encounter it — never again.
🗣️

Plain Language Always

No RPE numbers. No 1RM percentages. No jargon gatekeeping. The plain version works for everyone — the technical version doesn't.

  • Load guidance uses sensations, not abstract numbers
  • Enforced at the AI level — the system prompt prohibits jargon in all responses
The difference
"RPE 7–8 @ 72% 1RM for 3 sets of 8."
"Pick a weight where the last 2 reps feel genuinely hard — but your form still holds."
🌊

Cycle-Aware by Default

Cycle-phase awareness is a baseline assumption, not a feature toggle. There if she tracks, invisible if she doesn't. Never a selling point — just how good programming works for women.

  • Optional — programme works fully without it, gets smarter with it
  • Adjustments modify intensity and volume, not exercise selection
  • Banners are contextual cues, not instructions — "Energy often peaks this week" not "Push harder"
The difference
Marketing "period-proof workouts" or a toggle buried in settings.
"Oestrogen is rising — this is when your body responds to training most effectively." A quiet layer of intelligence.
🏗️

Structure: Context → Gap → Action

Every education piece follows the same arc. Two sentences max. Here's what's happening, here's what most people miss, here's what to do about it.

  • Context — reframe what the muscle or movement actually does
  • Gap — name what most people miss, without blame
  • Action — one concrete thing you can do with that information
"Always first when you're freshest — the squat is the most demanding movement in the session and everything else is built around the work it creates."
⚡

Always Delivers a Session

She opened the app to train. Whatever happens — API failure, network drop, edge case — she gets a session. Never an error wall. Never "try again later."

  • Hardcoded fallback programmes for every split and experience level — real sessions, not stubs
  • AI failure shows a full session with an amber notice, not a blank screen
The difference
"Something went wrong. Please try again later." She's already at the gym.
"Your session is ready" with a small amber dot. She never knows the difference.
🎨

Design That Doesn't Shout

Rose Quartz on near-black. Bebas Neue display, DM Sans 300 body. Border highlights only — never fill. Every screen whispers quality.

  • Dark-first palette for gym lighting — per-category colour accents (Rose/Violet/Gold/Sage)
  • No neon, no gradients, no competing colours — the content is the interface
The app looks like it belongs in a design portfolio — not a fitness app store listing.
🤝

Meet You Where You Are

Experience gates filter exercises based on confidence, not calendar time. A beginner never sees advanced movements. An experienced lifter isn't insulted with progressions she's outgrown.

  • Three tiers: "Still learning the basics" → "Comfortable with the main lifts" → "Confident programming my own training"
  • Equipment and injury filters ensure you only see exercises you can safely do
The difference
Everyone sees the same exercise list. Beginners are overwhelmed. Advanced lifters scroll past basics.
Your exercise library is curated to your level, equipment, and body — from day one.
📉

Progressive Autonomy

The app evolves with you — not away from you. Education thins as you mature and the relationship shifts from guidance to partnership. You don't outgrow Kinē; you graduate to using it differently.

  • Briefs: expanded (wk 1–2) → collapsed (wk 3–4) → reduced (wk 5–11) → graduated (wk 12+)
  • Three manual modes (full / feel / silent) let you control the pace yourself
The difference
Apps that keep you dependent — or apps you outgrow and delete.
Early: "You hit all 8 reps — time to add weight." Later: "All reps hit. Add weight." Established: "+2.5kg"
📊

Failure as Data

Missed sessions, stalled lifts, and gaps are signal — not failure. No streaks, no guilt, no denominators. Every return is met with a plan, not a lecture.

  • The app never counts what you didn't do — "2 sessions done", never "2 of 4"
  • Stalls reframed as consolidation. Gaps trigger a welcome-back card, not a guilt trip
The difference
"You completed 2 of 4 sessions this week. Try harder next week!"
"Same weight 3 weeks running. That's consolidation — your body is building the base for the next jump."
💪

Body Trust

Your body is competent until proven otherwise. Sensation is informative. Effort is genuine. Slow progress is still progress.

  • Energy shifts are contextualised, not diagnosed. Low energy: validate, adjust — never question
  • No before/after framing. No body composition language that implies a deficit to close
The difference
"Your hormones are affecting your performance this week."
"Energy and strength often shift this week. The programme accounts for it."
🔧

Minimum Effective Change

When adapting your programme — swaps, deloads, cycle adjustments — the app changes the least amount possible. One variable at a time. Your programme always feels like yours.

  • Swaps replace one movement with the closest equivalent. Deloads reduce load or volume, not both
  • Profile updates trigger targeted rebuilds, never full regeneration
The difference
Swap out bench press, get an entirely new push session you don't recognise.
Swap one exercise, keep everything else. Your session stays recognisable.
🔍

Transparency of Reasoning

You can always understand why your programme looks the way it does. No hidden logic. No black-box decisions.

  • Session and week education explains exercise ordering, split logic, and recovery sequencing
  • Deloads, swaps, and progression changes all explain what changed and why
The difference
"Deload week." No explanation. Feels like going backwards.
"Load drops but movement patterns stay. This is where the last three weeks convert into strength."
🛌

Recovery is Training

The session is the trigger. Recovery is where adaptation actually happens. Rest, sleep, and nutrition are part of training — not obstacles to it.

  • Rest days labelled "Recovery" — never "Rest" or "Off" — and carry a visible message
  • Deload weeks framed as earned recovery, not reduced training
The difference
Rest days shown as empty grey boxes — implying nothing is happening.
"The session triggered the adaptation. Sleep, food, and rest finish the work."
🚫

No Vanity Metrics

No streaks, badges, leaderboards, or XP. Motivation from a number on a screen is borrowed — and creates dependency.

  • "Weeks In" replaces streaks. No denominators. Calendar empty days carry no visual weight
  • Progress anchors to capability: "You squatted 60kg — three weeks ago it was 50kg"
The difference
"🔥 12-day streak! Don't break it!"
"Week 4 of your programme." Quiet. Factual. No pressure.
🕰️

Age-Adaptive Recovery

The AI silently adjusts session spacing, deload frequency, and exercise selection based on age — without ever mentioning it.

  • 35+: extra warm-up sets. 40+: 48h between same muscles, deload every 3–4 weeks. 50+: joint-friendly variations, no plyometrics
  • Age is never referenced in any user-facing copy — the programme just fits
The difference
"At your age, you should consider lighter weights."
A 48-year-old gets goblet squats and trap-bar deadlifts. The app never says why — it just programmes what fits.
👋

Specificity Is the Personal Touch

Personalisation isn't using her name — it's showing her the app knows her. The difference between "Good session" and "Your deadlift has moved up 10kg in 6 weeks" is specificity.

  • Real personalisation is in the data: load trends, feedback patterns, cycle timing, exercise history
  • AI copy references her specific history, not generic encouragement
The difference
"Great job, Sarah!" — generic praise with a name pasted in.
"Your squat has been building steadily — 45kg felt easy last week, try 47.5kg." That's personal.
🧭

Reflection Without Judgement

The weekly check-in exists to orient, not evaluate. A compass, not a scorecard.

  • Summaries focus on what was done — never what was missed. Two tappable questions, no free text
  • Input shapes next week's programme — it's not decorative
The difference
"2 of 4 sessions completed. You missed Tuesday and Thursday."
"Your deadlift is moving well and the intensity felt about right. Nice work."
🤲

Earn the Ask

Every piece of personal data must justify itself in one sentence. If it doesn't visibly improve the experience, we don't ask.

  • Onboarding collects only email and name — nothing else gates getting started
  • Optional fields show a one-line benefit: "Helps us adjust recovery to suit you"
The difference
A 12-field registration form before you've seen a single screen.
Every field earns its place with a visible reason to share.
🔒

Closed Loop by Design

Kinē is a standalone coaching relationship — not a node in a fitness ecosystem. No wearable sync, no social feed, no integrations. You are the sensor.

  • No inbound integrations. Share is one-way out — export a PR card if you choose, never nudged
  • Self-reported effort is as accurate as wearable data for training adjustment — without the noise
The difference
"Your Garmin says recovered. Your Whoop says not. What do you trust?"
You tell us how you feel. We programme from that. Two taps, no conflicting dashboards.
📚

Education is the Engagement

Knowledge is the retention loop — not gamification, not social pressure. "What will I learn today" is what makes sessions interesting beyond lifting.

  • Every session contains at least one piece of education you haven't seen before
  • As education thins, remaining content becomes higher-value: programming logic, periodisation reasoning
The difference
Apps that keep you coming back with streaks and badges.
Session 12: "Your hamstrings work hardest in the bottom half — that's why the first rep from the floor always feels heaviest."
🧠

Compounding Intelligence

Every interaction makes the next one smarter. The longer you use Kinē, the more precisely it understands you.

  • Session feedback, load history, and cycle data all feed into next-week AI generation
  • Programme changes carry forward — the app never resets what it knows about you
The difference
Generic programmes that feel the same at week 12 as week 1.
Week 8: suggestion shaped by your 7-week trend, cycle phase timing, and effort scores — without you doing anything differently.
🧭

You Shouldn't Have to Figure This Out Alone

The emotional problem isn't "I want to get fit" — it's "I don't know what to do." One decision: show up. The app handles the rest.

  • No blank states — the app always has a plan ready
  • Swaps, deloads, and cycle adjustments feel like being taken care of, not disrupted
The difference
A programme builder that asks you to choose exercises, set schemes, and progression.
Monday morning: session ready. Four exercises, weight guidance, a session brief. Just start.
🔮

Show Her the Investment

The app gets smarter over time — but that only matters if she can feel it. Specificity that wouldn't be possible without her history.

  • The app never says "based on your data" — it just becomes more specific
  • Programme restarts carry forward. Starting fresh doesn't mean starting from scratch
The difference
"You have 47 sessions logged!" (vanity metric)
Your session brief references the squat plateau from week 6 and the volume change that broke it. A new app couldn't do that.
🧭

Guide, Don't Gate

The app provides context — it never blocks, scores, or grades. When she changes her programme, the app observes what that change does. It doesn't tell her what to do.

  • No integrity scores or traffic-light ratings. Swaps show what changed, not whether it was right
  • Silence when everything's fine. A quiet note when something matters. Never a warning
The difference
"Session integrity: 38%. Your session balance is poor."
"This session is now all anterior (front of body)." Observation, not verdict.
♀️

Built on Women's Physiology

Calibrated for how women's bodies actually respond — not scaled-down male programming.

  • Women recover faster from upper body work — sessions can include more volume without compromise
  • Balance focuses on anterior vs posterior. Quads and hamstrings/glutes treated as complementary, not competing
The difference
Same rules for everyone — rest periods, volume caps, and progression designed around male physiology.
Women can push harder on upper body days, recover differently across the month, and benefit from programming that reflects their actual biology.
⚖️

Proportional Response

The app's reaction matches the scale of what happened. Silence is the default state, not an oversight.

  • A missed week gets a quiet re-entry plan. A PR gets acknowledged with specificity — not confetti
  • A good session gets a warm close — not "AMAZING WORK!!" energy
The difference
"🎉 INCREDIBLE SESSION! You're CRUSHING IT! 🔥💪"
"Deadlift moved to 70kg — that's 10kg more than 6 weeks ago." Specific. Grounded. Real.

🌊 Cycle Phase Integration

Phase boundaries scale dynamically to each user's average cycle length. After sufficient sessions, personalised phase-performance correlations feed into the AI.

Days 1–5
Menstrual
Oestrogen and progesterone at their lowest. Energy and strength typically reduced.
Strength: Hold loads, technique refinement. No new PRs.
Body: Keep achievable, muscle connection focus.
Habit: A shorter or lighter session still counts — completing it is the only goal.
Days 6–13
Follicular
Oestrogen rising rapidly — best adaptation window. Strength, power, recovery elevated.
Strength: Push loads and attempt PRs. Lead with specific load targets.
Body: Prime development window. Push load and connection.
Habit: She'll feel more energised — but don't increase intensity. Keep the habit sustainable.
Days 14–15
Ovulatory
Oestrogen peaks — power output highest, but joint laxity peaks. Best for strength PRs; caution on plyometrics.
Strength: Peak strength. Good window for PRs.
Body: Push load and connection. Peak energy available.
Habit: Energy is high but the programme stays the same — consistency over peaks.
Days 16–28
Luteal
Progesterone dominant. Endurance capacity better but peak strength reduced. Higher body temp, fatigue accumulates faster.
Strength: Hold loads, no new PRs. Technique focus.
Body: Prioritise form and feel over weight. Development continues at lower intensity.
Habit: Lower energy is normal. A completed session at any intensity counts as a win.

🗣️ Programme Voice by Goal

The AI system prompt enforces a different personality per goal. Same exercises, completely different framing.

Serious Strength
The bar weight is the measure. Every session brief leads with target weight and what a good set feels like. Short and specific — lead with load, not encouragement. Never frame progress through aesthetics.
"Squat target is 5kg above last week. Last two reps should be hard — form holds but it is a fight. Anything easier means add weight."
A Body I Feel Good In
Connect every exercise to the whole picture. Upper back work changes shoulder-to-waist ratio. Side shoulder work creates width. Hip hinge and glute as primary movements, not accessories. Load increases framed as development, not performance.
"Romanian deadlifts aren't just a hamstring exercise — they shape the entire back of your body. Slow the lowering and feel the stretch."
A Habit That Actually Lasts
Every session should feel achievable and leave her wanting to come back. Session briefs celebrate showing up, not performance numbers. Never push intensity that risks making the next session daunting.
"Three sessions this week. The pattern is forming — that is the work."

All voice outputs are governed by the Key Principles above — Body Trust, No Guilt, Recovery is Training, Minimum Effective Change, Guide Don't Gate, Women's Physiology, Proportional Response, Age-Adaptive, and Reflection Without Judgement all apply to every AI-generated text.

🔥 Dynamic Warm-up Engine

Every session opens with a warm-up built from what you're about to do — not a generic routine.

1
General Prep — 2 movements from the session's primary muscle focus (hip circles for legs day, thoracic rotations for push day)
2
Activation — 1 targeted activation per focus group (max 3). Clamshells before squats, face pulls before pressing, lat activation before deadlifts.
3
Stabiliser Extras — Exercise-specific prep. Barbell Back Squat triggers lat bracing drill. Front Squat triggers wrist and lat prep. Deadlift triggers lat engagement cue.
4
Injury Mods — Up to 2 protective movements based on flagged injuries. Shoulder issues add pendulum swings. Knee concerns add terminal knee extensions.
5
Ramp-up Sets — Compounds only. New users get 2 ramp sets, developing get 3, intermediate get 4 — scaling from empty bar to working weight.

🔄 Injury-Aware Swaps

When an injury is flagged, exercises are automatically swapped for safer alternatives that preserve session balance.

🔙 Lower Back
Deadlift → Trap Bar Deadlift, RDL → Glute Bridge, Barbell Row → Cable Row
💪 Shoulder
OHP → Lateral Raise, Bench → DB Bench, Pull-Up → Lat Pulldown, Dips → Cable Fly
✋ Wrist
Barbell Bench → DB Bench, Barbell Row → Cable Row, Push-Up → DB Bench
🦴 Hip

🦶 Ankle
Walking Lunge → Step Up, Box Jump → Step Up, Calf Raise → Seated Calf Raise

Each swap is scored for session integrity (0–100). "Ideal" swaps match muscle group and session focus. "Compromise" swaps flag potential imbalances.

📚 Education System

Micro-learning that surfaces once, at the right moment, then graduates when you don't need it anymore.

Three education modes — Full (weeks 1-4): collapsible blocks with detail. Feel (reduced): breathing and muscle focus only. Silent: education layer hidden entirely. Mode adapts to programme maturity.
Week 12 graduation — After 12 weeks, most education cues retire automatically. Breathing cues and the "?" muscle sheet remain available. The app trusts that you've learned.
DOMS education — Five-tier contextual: first sessions get reassurance, moderate soreness gets normalisation, high soreness gets recovery advice, experienced users (8+ weeks) get trust-based messaging, and no-soreness users learn adaptation is working.
Muscle glossary — 13 muscles with training context and "trained by" exercise lists. Exercises in your current programme are highlighted. Tap-to-learn from any exercise card.
Anatomy cues — 21 exercises with body-feedback signs ("Knees caving inward?") and context-specific fixes. Tap-to-reveal during sessions.
Interaction tracking — Developer-only counters track education opens, muscle explainer views, and anatomy cue interactions via progressDB.eduInteractions.

🪝 Hook Model

Four retention mechanisms wired into the session lifecycle — each creates a reason to return without resorting to notifications or guilt. All four are goal-aware: strength users see load-focused rewards, muscle users see volume signals, and habit users see consistency wins.

Variable session insights — A rotating pool of data-driven observations shown post-session: total volume, volume vs last same-type session, heaviest set, and programme totals. Deterministic rotation ensures you see something different each time. Insight pool is reordered by goal — strength leads with heaviest set, muscle with volume, habit with streaks.
Session bridge — Post-session recovery card shows the next session title with a progression teaser ("Next: Upper Push — bench press moving to 42.5kg"). Week view mirrors this with a one-line teaser on the next upcoming session. Habit-goal users see remaining sessions for the week instead of a progression teaser. Every session ending plants a hook for the next.
Mastery milestones — Celebration logic detects three wins beyond PRs: weight graduation (moved up after 2+ weeks at the same load), rep mastery (first time hitting all target reps at a weight), and consistency streaks (meaningful intervals). One milestone per session, rarest first. Milestone priority reorders by goal — strength leads with weight graduation, muscle with rep mastery, habit with consistency.
Stored value at re-entry — After 7+ days away, a welcome-back card surfaces real data: total sessions, weeks trained, and top lift progressions. No motivational fluff — just proof of what you've built. The app reminds you of your investment without guilting you for the gap.

All four mechanisms use real training data from progressDB — nothing fabricated. Goal-awareness reads the onboarding goal from the user's profile.
