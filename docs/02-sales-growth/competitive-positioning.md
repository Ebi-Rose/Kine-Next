# Competitive Positioning

How Kinē differs from every other training app on the market.

## The One-Liner

**Kinē is AI-powered strength programming built on women's physiology — not a workout logger with a pink theme.**

## The Problem

Women who want to train seriously have two options:
1. **Generic apps** (Hevy, Strong, JEFIT) — built for men, adapted with a colour change. No cycle awareness, no women's physiology, no intelligent programming.
2. **A coach** — £150-400/month. Great if you can afford it and find a good one. Most can't or don't.

There's nothing in between. No tool that actually programs for women's bodies — with periodisation, cycle-phase awareness, and progressive overload — without requiring a human coach.

Kinē fills that gap.

## Why We Win

### 1. We're not a logger — we're a programmer
Hevy, Strong, and JEFIT are **digital notebooks**. You still need to know what to do. Kinē tells you what to do, adjusts weekly, and explains why.

### 2. Women's physiology is the foundation, not a feature
Other apps treat women's training as "men's training minus the ego lifts." Kinē is built from the ground up around:
- Posterior chain priority (glutes, hamstrings, upper back)
- Higher volume tolerance at lower relative intensities
- Cycle-phase energy and recovery patterns
- Injury patterns common in women (knee valgus, shoulder instability)

### 3. No social, no noise
Kine is a closed loop. No feeds, no followers, no comparison. This is intentional — social features in fitness apps consistently increase anxiety and churn, especially for women.

### 4. AI that knows context
Fitbod uses AI to pick exercises. Juggernaut uses AI for periodisation. Kinē does both, AND incorporates cycle phase, injury history, equipment constraints, and progressive overload — in a single generation.

### 5. Price reflects value
At £29.99/month, Kinē costs more than loggers — because it does more. It costs a fraction of coaching — because it should. Users who'd never pay for a coach will pay for a tool that does 80% of what a coach does.

## Target User

**Primary:** Women aged 22-40 who:
- Want to train with weights (not just cardio or yoga)
- Have some gym experience but don't know how to program effectively
- Can't afford or don't want a personal coach
- Value privacy and don't want a social fitness platform
- May track their menstrual cycle and want training to reflect it

**Secondary:** Women who currently use a logger (Hevy/Strong) and have outgrown "just tracking" — they want intelligence behind their training.

**Not our user:** Complete beginners who need in-person form coaching, competitive powerlifters/bodybuilders (use sport-specific tools), people who want a social fitness experience.
