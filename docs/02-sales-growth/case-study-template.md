## Collection Guidelines

### When to ask
- After 4+ weeks of consistent use
- After a milestone (PR, streak, completed a full programme block)
- When a user sends unprompted positive feedback (capture immediately)

### How to ask
Keep it conversational. Don't send a formal survey. Instead:

> "Hey [name] — you've been using Kinē for [X weeks] now and it looks like things are going well. Would you mind sharing a bit about your experience? Totally optional, and I can keep it anonymous if you prefer. Just a few questions about what training was like before and what's changed."

### What to capture
- **Always get a direct quote** — even one sentence is gold
- **Ask for permission** to use their words (anonymously or named)
- **Get specific numbers** where possible — "stronger" is good, "hip thrust went from 40kg to 70kg" is better
- **Ask what almost stopped them** — objections they overcame are powerful for marketing

### What NOT to do
- Don't write the case study for them and ask them to approve it
- Don't cherry-pick only dramatic transformations — steady, sustainable progress is more credible
- Don't use before/after photos (not our brand)
- Don't exaggerate or embellish their words

## Example Case Study (Hypothetical)

### Sarah, 28 — London

**Profile:** 3 years of gym, mostly following Instagram workouts. Used Strong for logging. Goal: build glutes and overall strength.

**Before:** "I'd go to the gym 4 times a week and just do whatever I'd saved on Instagram. Some weeks I'd do legs three times and no upper body. I was consistent but I wasn't progressing."

**After 8 weeks with Kinē:**
- Sessions/week: 4 (unchanged — but now structured)
- Hip thrust: 50kg → 72.5kg
- First unassisted pull-up
- "I didn't realise how much I was neglecting my upper body and back. The AI just... balanced everything out. I didn't have to think about it."

**Would tell a friend:** "It's like having a coach who actually knows what they're doing, but you don't have to message them or wait for a reply."

**Key takeaway:** Users who train consistently but without structure see the fastest gains — Kinē doesn't add motivation, it adds intelligence.
