## Boilerplate (Long)

Kinē (from Greek κίνησις — movement) is a fitness programming tool that uses AI to generate personalised weekly strength training programs for women. Unlike generic training apps that serve as digital notebooks, Kinē actively programs your training — selecting exercises, prescribing sets, reps, and weights, and adjusting weekly based on your performance and recovery.

What sets Kinē apart is its foundation in women's physiology. The platform accounts for menstrual cycle phases, posterior chain development priorities, and recovery patterns specific to women — factors that mainstream fitness apps ignore entirely. Cycle tracking is optional and built-in, allowing programs to adapt to the user's energy and recovery patterns across their cycle.

Kinē is deliberately minimal: no social features, no feeds, no gamification. It's a focused tool that does one thing well — give women intelligent training programs that adapt to their bodies and their lives.

## FAQ for Press

**Q: Is this another AI fitness app?**
A: Most "AI fitness apps" use basic algorithms to pick exercises. Kinē uses Anthropic's Claude to generate complete, periodised weekly programs — factoring in goals, equipment, injuries, progressive overload, and menstrual cycle phase. It's closer to what a human coach does than what a randomiser does.

**Q: Why women-only?**
A: Because women's training has been underserved for decades. Most apps and research are built around male physiology. Women have different recovery patterns, hormonal cycles that affect training capacity, and different injury predispositions. Building specifically for women means we can do this properly rather than as an afterthought.

**Q: How does cycle tracking work?**
A: Users optionally log their period start dates. Kinē calculates the cycle phase and makes subtle adjustments to programming — not dramatic changes, but informed ones. For example, it might shift intensity during the luteal phase when energy typically dips. Users on hormonal birth control or with irregular cycles can adjust settings accordingly.

**Q: Is this safe?**
A: Kinē is a training programming tool, not a medical device. Programs account for user-reported injuries and limitations, but we always advise consulting a healthcare provider before starting any exercise program. The app includes a clear medical disclaimer.

**Q: Why no native app?**
A: Kinē is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it installs on your phone's home screen, works offline, and doesn't require app store distribution. This lets us ship updates instantly and keep pricing lower without app store fees. A native app is on the roadmap.
