# Content Style Guide

Specific rules for writing content within Kinē — exercise cues, coaching feedback, UI copy, and marketing.

This extends the Brand Voice Guide with tactical writing rules.

---

## General Rules

### Sentence case everywhere
- Headings: "Your weekly program" not "Your Weekly Program"
- Buttons: "Generate program" not "Generate Program"
- Labels: "Session duration" not "Session Duration"
- Exception: "Kinē" is always capitalised. "KINĒ" only in the logo.

### No trailing periods on UI elements
- Button: "Save session" (no period)
- Toast: "Session saved" (no period)
- Label: "Sets completed" (no period)
- Exception: Full sentences in body text, tooltips, and education content use periods.

### Numbers
- Use numerals for training data: "3 sets of 8 reps" not "three sets of eight reps"
- Use numerals for time: "90 seconds" not "ninety seconds"
- Use words for casual quantities: "a few exercises" not "3-5 exercises"
- Weight: always include unit — "60kg" not "60"

### Time formatting
- Duration: "45 min" not "45 minutes" or "0:45:00"
- Rest timer: "1:30" (minutes:seconds)
- Dates: "Mon 24 Mar" not "Monday, March 24th, 2026"

## Exercise Cues

### Structure
Each exercise has:
1. **Primary cue** (1 sentence — the most important thing)
2. **Setup** (2-3 bullet points — body position)
3. **Execution** (2-3 bullet points — movement)
4. **Common mistakes** (2-3 bullet points — what to avoid)

### Writing cues
- Use imperative voice: "Drive through your heels" not "You should drive through your heels"
- Be body-specific: "Squeeze your glutes at the top" not "Squeeze at the top"
- Avoid ambiguous directions: "Push the bar away from your chest" not "Push up"
- One action per cue: "Brace your core" not "Brace your core and drive through your heels"

### Examples

**Good cues:**
- "Hinge at the hips, not the lower back."
- "Keep your knees tracking over your toes."
- "Lower the bar to your mid-chest, elbows at 45 degrees."
- "Stand up by driving your feet into the floor."

**Bad cues:**
- "Do the movement correctly." (useless)
- "Activate your glutes, engage your core, keep your chest up, and drive through your heels." (too many things)
- "This exercise works your quads and glutes." (that's anatomy, not a cue)

## Coaching Feedback

### Tone
- Observational, not evaluative
- Brief — 1-2 sentences max
- Present a fact, then an implication

### Structure
[Observation] → [Implication or suggestion]

### Examples

**Good:**
- "You increased weight on hip thrust for 3 weeks straight. Strength is trending up."
- "Two sessions skipped this week. Next week's volume is adjusted."
- "Your push volume has been higher than pull. This week balances that out."

**Bad:**
- "Great job this week! You're doing amazing! Keep it up! 💪" (performative)
- "You only completed 2 of 4 sessions. Try harder next week." (guilt)
- "Based on your periodisation mesocycle analysis, your accumulated fatigue index suggests a deload." (jargon)

## Error Messages

### Structure
[What happened] + [What to do]

### Examples

**Good:**
- "Couldn't generate your program. Check your connection and try again."
- "Session didn't sync. It's saved locally — we'll try again when you're online."
- "Something went wrong. Try refreshing the page."

**Bad:**
- "Error 500: Internal server error" (meaningless to user)
- "Oops! Something went wrong 😅" (patronising)
- "The AI model returned an invalid JSON response that could not be parsed by the client-side handler." (too technical)

## Cycle-Related Copy

### Rules
- Never dramatise cycle effects: "your energy may be lower" not "you'll feel terrible"
- Frame as information, not restriction: "menstrual phase — Day 3" not "WARNING: menstrual phase"
- Always optional language: "Energy may be lower" not "Energy is lower"
- Never use "that time of the month" or similar euphemisms
- Be clinical where appropriate: "menstrual phase" not "period phase"

### Phase banners
- **Menstrual:** "Menstrual phase · Day [X]. Energy may be lower — listen to your body."
- **Follicular:** "Follicular phase · Day [X]. Good window for pushing intensity."
- **Ovulatory:** "Ovulatory phase · Day [X]. Peak performance window."
- **Luteal:** "Luteal phase · Day [X]. Recovery may take longer — the program accounts for this."

## Marketing Copy

### Landing page
- Lead with the outcome, not the feature
- "Programming that adapts to your cycle" not "AI-powered menstrual cycle integration"
- Use specific numbers: "188 exercises" not "hundreds of exercises"
- Comparison: "Smarter than a template. Cheaper than a coach." — position against alternatives

### CTAs
- "Start training" (not "Sign up" or "Get started" or "Join now")
- "See your program" (not "Try it free")
- "Manage subscription" (not "Go to billing")

### Testimonials (when we have them)
- Real quotes only — never fabricated or paraphrased
- Include context: "Sarah, 28 — training for 2 years"
- Focus on outcome: what changed, not just "I love it"

## Words We Use vs. Words We Don't

| Use | Don't Use | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Session | Workout | Structured work, not random effort |
| Training | Exercise | Intentional practice |
| Program | Routine | Designed, not habitual |
| Build muscle | Tone / sculpt | Honest about what's happening |
| Adjust / modify | Downgrade / scale back | Neutral, not diminishing |
| Recovery | Rest day | Active concept, not absence |
| Strength | Fitness | Specific over vague |
| Women | Ladies / girls / females | Respectful |
| Generate | Create / build (for AI) | Accurate — the AI generates |
| Suggest | Recommend / prescribe | We guide, not prescribe (Principle 2) |
