## Boilerplate (Long)

Kinē (from Greek κίνησις — movement) is a fitness programming tool that uses AI to generate personalised weekly strength training programs for women. Unlike generic training apps that serve as digital notebooks, Kinē actively programs your training — selecting exercises, prescribing sets, reps, and weights, and adjusting weekly based on your performance and recovery.

What sets Kinē apart is its foundation in women's physiology. The platform accounts for menstrual cycle phases, health conditions (PCOS, endometriosis, fibroids, pelvic floor dysfunction), life stages (perimenopause, postpartum), posterior chain development priorities, and recovery patterns specific to women — factors that mainstream fitness apps ignore entirely. These adaptations are silent and woven into existing programming, not surfaced as labels or special modes.

Kinē is deliberately minimal: no social features, no feeds, no gamification. It's a focused tool that does one thing well — give women intelligent training programs that adapt to their bodies and their lives.

## FAQ for Press

**Q: Is this another AI fitness app?**
A: Most "AI fitness apps" use basic algorithms to pick exercises. Kinē uses Anthropic's Claude to generate complete, personalised weekly programs — factoring in goals, equipment, injuries, progressive overload, cycle phase, and health conditions like PCOS or endometriosis. Every AI-generated program is validated against a 170+ exercise library with equipment, injury, and condition checks. It's closer to what a human coach does than what a randomiser does.

**Q: Why women-only?**
A: Because women's training has been underserved for decades. Most apps and research are built around male physiology. Women have different recovery patterns, hormonal cycles, and conditions — PCOS affects 1 in 10, endometriosis affects 1 in 10, pelvic floor dysfunction affects 40-55% of exercising women. No mainstream fitness app accounts for any of this. Building specifically for women means we can address these realities properly rather than as an afterthought.

**Q: How does the body-awareness system work?**
A: Kinē adapts to multiple dimensions of women's health. Cycle tracking is optional — users log period start dates and Kinē learns how *their* cycle affects *their* training over time, rather than applying blanket phase rules. Beyond cycle, users can flag conditions like PCOS, endometriosis, fibroids, or pelvic floor dysfunction. The programme silently adapts — swapping exercises, adjusting warmups, modifying breathing cues — without labelling the user or creating a "special mode." Users on hormonal contraception, with irregular cycles, or in perimenopause each get appropriately tailored programming.

**Q: Is this safe?**
A: Kinē is a training programming tool, not a medical device. Programs account for user-reported injuries and limitations, but we always advise consulting a healthcare provider before starting any exercise program. The app includes a clear medical disclaimer.

**Q: Why no native app?**
A: Kinē is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it installs on your phone's home screen, works offline, and doesn't require app store distribution. This lets us ship updates instantly and keep pricing lower without app store fees. A native app is on the roadmap.
