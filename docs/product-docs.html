Foundation

The 20 Principles

Every feature, change, and design decision is assessed against these. They are non-negotiable.

01

Built on Women's Physiology

Recovery, volume, balance calibrated for women. Not men's training scaled down.

02

Guide, Don't Gate

Observe, don't grade. No scores, no verdicts, no pass/fail.

03

Closed Loop

No integrations, no social. Self-report is the signal.

04

One Purpose Per Page

Every screen does one thing. Two things = two screens.

05

Progressive Autonomy

Smarter use, not more use. Build competence, not dependency.

06

Cycle Awareness Is Subtle

A feature, not the headline. Women are more than their cycles.

07

Messaging Reflects Reality

Never guilt. Never shame. State what happened, suggest what's next.

08

AI Is Infrastructure

Users think about their program, not the AI behind it.

09

Offline First

Works where people train — including gyms with no signal.

10

Privacy by Default

Minimum data. No trackers. Cycle data is health data.

11

Simplicity Over Completeness

188 curated exercises > 1,400 random ones.

12

About You Is Minimal

Name, weight, height. That's it for personal info.

13

Per-Day Control

Tuesday's 45 min lunch session ≠ Saturday's 90 min session.

14

No Scores, No Streaks

Don't gamify training. Show progress through real metrics.

15

Session, Not Workout

Language matters. Structured work, not random movement.

16

Exercise Education

Don't just say what — explain why and how.

17

Rest Is Programming

Deloads and recovery prescribed as confidently as heavy sets.

18

Equipment Reality

Bodyweight-only users get thoughtful programs, not consolation prizes.

19

Injuries Are Constraints

A bad knee narrows the pool — it doesn't stop programming.

20

The User Is the Authority

Kine suggests. The user decides. No judgment.
