# Kinē Brand & Voice Guide

## Brand Identity

**Kinē** (from Greek κίνησις — movement) is a fitness programming tool built specifically for women. It removes the friction between wanting to train and actually doing it.

### What Kinē Is
- A flexible system that adapts to your life
- An intelligent programming tool, not a social network
- Built on women's physiology, not scaled-down men's training
- A guide, not a gatekeeper

### What Kinē Is Not
- A coach replacement (it's a tool)
- A fitness influencer app (no social, no feeds)
- A calorie tracker or diet app
- A generic workout generator with a pink theme

## Voice Principles

### 1. Calm, Not Clinical
Write like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook. Avoid medical jargon unless necessary, and when it is necessary, explain it.

**Yes:** "Your program shifts focus this week — your body recovers differently in this phase."
**No:** "Luteal phase detected. Reducing volume by 15% based on hormonal fluctuation models."

### 2. Direct, Not Bossy
State things clearly. Don't hedge, don't over-explain, don't use filler.

**Yes:** "3 sets of 8. Rest 90 seconds."
**No:** "We recommend that you might want to try doing approximately 3 sets of around 8 repetitions, resting for about 90 seconds between sets if that feels comfortable for you!"

### 3. Encouraging, Not Performative
Support without cheerleading. Never guilt. Never shame.

**Yes:** "You trained 3 days this week. Here's next week."
**No:** "Amazing job crushing it this week! 💪🔥 You're a WARRIOR!"

### 4. Honest, Not Preachy
If something matters, say it plainly. Don't lecture.

**Yes:** "This is a rest week. Your body needs it to actually get stronger."
**No:** "Remember, rest is JUST as important as training! Many people make the mistake of..."

### 5. Inclusive, Not Patronising
Write for women who train seriously. Don't dumb things down. Don't assume ignorance.

**Yes:** "Hip thrust — 4×10 @ RPE 7"
**No:** "Hip thrust — this is a great exercise for your glutes (that's your bum muscles!)"

## Banned Words & Phrases

| Don't Say | Why |
|---|---|
| "Tone", "toning" | Meaningless. Say "build muscle" or "get stronger." |
| "Beast mode", "crush it", "slay" | Performative fitness culture we're deliberately avoiding. |
| "No excuses" | Guilt-based. We accommodate life, not shame it. |
| "Bikini body", "summer ready" | Appearance-driven framing. Kine is about capability. |
| "Simple", "easy" | Training isn't easy. Don't minimise the work. |
| "Just" (as minimiser) | "Just do 5 sets" — the word "just" dismisses effort. |
| "Ladies" | Dated. Say "women" or address the user directly. |
| "Workout" (as a noun) | Use "session" or "training" — positions it as structured work. |
| "Algorithm" | Users don't care how. Say "your program" or "Kinē." |

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | Warm, clear, one-thing-at-a-time | "What's your main goal?" |
| Session logging | Minimal, functional, stay out of the way | "Set 3 of 4 · 60kg" |
| Coaching nudges | Brief, observational, no judgment | "Volume's been high — next week pulls back." |
| Error states | Honest, helpful, calm | "Couldn't generate your program. Check your connection and try again." |
| Cycle banners | Informative, not prescriptive | "Menstrual phase · Day 3. Energy may be lower — listen to your body." |
| Marketing/landing | Confident, specific, no hype | "AI-powered programming built on women's physiology." |
| Support replies | Empathetic, solution-first | "That sounds frustrating. Here's what's happening and how to fix it." |

## Typography & Visual Tone

- **Headings:** Bebas Neue — bold, architectural, no-nonsense
- **Body:** DM Sans Light (300) — clean, modern, easy to read
- **Accent colour:** #C49098 (muted rose — warm without being "girly")
- **Background:** #111111 (near-black — premium, focused)
- **Surfaces:** #191919 / #212121 (subtle depth, not flat)

The visual design should feel like a premium tool, not a lifestyle app. Think: Notion meets a powerlifting gym.

## Writing Checklist

Before publishing any copy, check:

- [ ] Is it saying one thing? (Not three things disguised as one)
- [ ] Would you say this to someone mid-set? (Keep it that focused)
- [ ] Does it respect the user's intelligence?
- [ ] Is it free of banned words?
- [ ] Does it guide without gating? (Observe, don't grade)
- [ ] Could it be shorter? (It probably can)
